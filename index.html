Performance Insights

Performance Distribution

There is considerable variation in student performance, with some students consistently achieving perfect scores while others struggle with many exercises. The top performers (like 1001 and 1002) maintain high average scores above 80%, while the overall average success rate is 42.35%.

Time Efficiency

The data shows that spending more time on exercises does not necessarily correlate with better performance (correlation: -0.0842). Some students achieve high scores with relatively little time spent on exercises, suggesting efficient problem-solving strategies.

Difficulty Patterns

There is significant variation in exercise difficulty, with success rates ranging from 7.25% to 97.23%. The exercises in the Ü3A1 series appear to be particularly challenging, with consistently low success rates.

Time-Difficulty Relationship

The most time-consuming exercises also tend to be among the most difficult ones. For example, Ü4A2 requires an average of 45.32 minutes and has a success rate of only 12.35%. This suggests that these exercises involve more complex concepts or calculations.

Time-Difficulty Relationship

Feedback Insights

Counterintuitive Finding

The analysis reveals an unexpected pattern: exercises with feedback have lower average scores (53.22) than those without feedback (68.45). This difference of 15.23 points is statistically significant.

Interpretation

This counterintuitive finding likely reflects that feedback is primarily provided for incorrect answers or more challenging exercises, rather than indicating that feedback negatively impacts performance. In other words, feedback is reactive rather than proactive in this dataset.

Recommendations

For Instructors

  • Focus additional instructional resources on the most challenging topics identified in this analysis
  • Consider introducing intermediate exercises that bridge the gap between the easiest and most difficult problems
  • Review the time requirements for the most time-consuming exercises
  • Implement more proactive feedback mechanisms that guide students during the problem-solving process

For Students

  • Pay special attention to the concepts covered in the most challenging exercises
  • Focus on quality of engagement rather than quantity of time spent
  • Seek feedback proactively, not just when struggling
  • Study the strategies of top-performing students

For Curriculum Development

  • Analyze the characteristics of exercises with high success rates to identify effective problem design patterns
  • Consider breaking down complex, time-consuming exercises into smaller, more manageable components
  • Develop a more balanced distribution of exercise difficulty
  • Create more opportunities for proactive feedback
